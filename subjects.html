CIT25: Enterprise Networking Security and Automation

This course primarily focused on accessing Wide Area Networks (WAN).

The goal is to develop understanding of various WAN technologies

to connect small to medium-sized business networks. Finally, students learn how to detect, troubleshoot and correct

common Enterprise network implementation issues.

CIT3: IT Project Management

CIT3 provides a foundation in managing information

technology projects. The course covers essential project

management processes such as planning, scheduling, budgeting,

and risk management. It emphasizes teamwork, leadership,

and communication skills, preparing students to successfully

guide IT projects from initiation to completion while balancing

scope, time, and resources.

CC19: Data Mining

This course introduces students to the fundamental concepts,

techniques, and tools of data mining. You will learn how to

extract meaningful patterns, trends, and relationships from

large datasets to support informed decision-making. By the

end of the course, you’ll gain practical skills to analyze

data effectively and apply data mining methods

in real-world scenarios.

CC14: Web Application Development

CC14 introduces students to the principles and practices

of building modern web applications. The course explores

front-end and back-end development, focusing on technologies

like HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Students learn to design

responsive, user-friendly interfaces and apply development

frameworks to create functional, efficient, and scalable web

solutions.
